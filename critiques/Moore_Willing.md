Books: Birds of America

Story: Willing

Author: Lorrie Moore 

Impression: One of those stories that makes you want to read it again because it ends with a confusing ending. The language was quick and wavering, uneasy, uncertain, jumping around. 

Now her hands trembled too much, even drinking juice, *especially* drinking juice, a Vantage wobbling between her fingers like a compass dial. She was sent scripts in which she was supposed to say lines she would never say, not wear clothes she would never not wear.

And so she left Hollywood. Phoned her agent and apologized. Went home to Chicago, rented a room by the week at the Days Inn, drank sherry, and grew a little plump. 

Structure: A washed-up actress from LA moves back 'home' to Chicago and wavers. She has long-distance phone calls with her friend from LA (connection to past life) and meets a guy car mechanic (connection to new life). 


But she was willing. Willing to give it a whirl. Once in a while, though she tried not to, she asked him about children, about having children, about turning kith to kin. How did he feel about all that? It seemed to her that if she were ever going to have a life of children and lawn mowers and grass clippings, it would be best to have it with someone who was not demeaned or trivialized by discussions with them. Did he like those big fertilized lawns? How about a nice rock garden? How did he feel deep down about those combination storm windows wit hthe built-in screens?
